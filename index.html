Time,
Measured

A watch does not tell you the time. It keeps it — against friction, against gravity, against the slow insistence of entropy. Six hundred hours of assembly, for a movement you will rarely look at.

Steel is not a luxury. Steel is a promise: that this object will outlast the hand that winds it, and the one after that. Precision is the only ornament we permit ourselves.

The Case

Cut from a single billet of surgical steel, then finished by hand across nine stages. The bezel alone takes a full day. Every surface is either mirror-polished or brushed — never both by accident.

The Movement

Calibre O-1. Hand-wound, twenty-one thousand six hundred vibrations per hour, seventy-two hours of reserve. Visible through sapphire, because a thing made this carefully has nothing to hide.

Kept,
Not Owned

You do not buy a watch like this. You hold it for a while, wind it every morning, and pass it on. Ownership is a formality. Custody is the point.
